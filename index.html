About Me

My name is Kristen Michelle Van Neste and I am a software engineer.

Prior to starting my career as a web developer, I had the incredible experience of working as an Associate Certified Wildlife Biologist. I studied a wide range of wildlife, from bats in South Carolina to mountain lions in Los Angeles. My experiences as a biologist introduced me to technologies such as GIS, database management, and software development. After five years of field work I realized I was on the wrong career path - I turned down a funded Master's position at the University of Wisconsin and started studying web development. Now, I work as the lead Front-End Developer at the non-profit Conservation Science Partners (CSP).

At CSP, I specialize in building web map applications using JavaScript, React, MapboxGL JS, and Deck.GL. Within my first year I published three web applications for Microsoft promoting the release of the Planetary Computer, and am in the process of completing web apps for American Farmland Trust, Bureau of Land Management, and the North Central Climate Adaptation Science Center. Working closely with data scientists and engineers has exposed me to tools and languages such as Python, Google App Engine, Azure, Docker, Tippecanoe, and Google Analytics.

As a proactive learner, I am always open to new opportunities to further develop my career.
